Macbooks Make Their Debut At Walmart: An All Time Price Low

04.03.24

Walmart is selling the Macbook Air M1 for the first time ever, and the catch: At an all time price low of $699. Immediately we're asking why? To whose advantage is this? After a little bit of digging I think I found the answer.

Both Apple and Walmart will mututally benefit from this deal. Apple took down the M1 laptops recently to make way for the M3s. It was a little disheartening to see them go. The M1 was the OG wedge shape. But my heartache was premature. Later in the week Walmart made their announcement.

Now Apple gets to continue to sell their product at a lower price without hurting their premium brand, and Walmart gets to tap into that premium market. The partnership has made major headlines offering both parties a slew of bonus advertising.

Apple is a premium brand. Walmart is for the people. If Apple chose to sell this same model on their website for $699 how do you think that would affect their overall branding? A new Macbook Air M3 on their website starts at $999. To say the least it wouldn't look good. And what's more Best Buy now is offering this same laptop for $649. For the average person who wants to finally own a macbook this is a pretty good place to start. 256GB of storage and 8GB RAM is perfect for the everyday user. As Bob Dylan once said "The times they are a changin", he's not wrong. And I think it's safe to say they are changing for the better. The link to the deal is here.
