Chess Is Helping Sharpen My Problem Solving Skills

03.29.24

Problem Solving is a skill that all human beings live and die by. It's probably one of the most important skills we can have, considering it's applicable across all facets of our everyday existence. How is problem solving taught? How do we learn to develop our critical-thinking? As a child we play games to help sharpen these skills. It starts with the matching games where you flip over a card and then try to remember where it's twin is lying in the grid. That directly translates to laundry day and matching one sock to the other. Glad you played those games right? But there are other games too. As a kid, we had an old Russian chess set from my Grandma. It sat in the sun room always readily available. At the time I wasn't aware of it's importance, it was only later I realized why my dad was always pushing to play. Well for starters, it's fun. Also not to mention being able to strut around the house and claim victory over the last game was a medal of honor. Looking back I've come to see the underlying values the game has to offer. One on one. No teammates to blame, no coach telling you what to do, it is you and yourself against your opponent. is a skill we start to develop at a very young age.
