ABOUTME

Hello, my name is Jude Shuma. I am an inherently curious individual from Chicago, IL. I am proud to say I have a bachelors degree in English Literature. My life has been far from convention which is probably why I am here writing this now. I have pursued art for the majority of my adult life. At one point I was working four different jobs on a weekly basis to fund the build out of my recording studio. I still own and operate that studio to this day. I have written music for commercials and movies. Landed my songs on multiple major American Television Networks. I even learned to daytrade the stock market to allow more time and freedom to make art. I found later that the stress outweighed the reward...not for me. But hey it was a great learning experience. I have found love by walking up to a complete stranger 9 years ago. I even taught myself her language (Georgian) და ეხლა შემიძლია დავწერო და წავიკითხო ქართულად. I was desperate to be done with school my whole life only to find I will spend my entire life studying. Poke around, send me an email, I love connecting with new people.

 

I decided after much debate to just start over. I kicked my Wordpress and purchased a Linux server. I wanted to build a website from nothing. My journey in music production has been nothing short of "Figure it out as I go". And programming has a lot of parallels in that regard. This site has been built on HTML, CSS, Vanilla Javascript, Ubuntu, Apache, MySQL, PHP, DNS, Node JS, Bootstrap, SSH, SCP and SSL. Somewhere along the way I came up with the idea for Chordo.

Which lead me to learning Python3, then Flask, WSGI... It's like I knew what I was getting into...but was also forcing a bit of naivety. I started to write about all of this because I love writing, so why not? Maybe I'll tap into some of those old psychology books while I'm at it. Right though, back to the code stuff. Programming is a lot like music. You start creating something and then you break it down. There's harmony in there somewhere just like there's harmony in one's code. Everything needs to fit just right. Microscopic details can't go unnoticed. Oh and also like music, it's a whole lot of back and forthing. Like a whole whole lot -- bunch of back and forthing.
